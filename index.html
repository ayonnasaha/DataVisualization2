Migrant Population in Australia

Migration around the world

In recent years, migrant populations across the world reached unprecedented levels, with millions of people living outside their country of birth. This map visualizes the migrant populations of various countries in millions, showing how nations such as the United States, Germany, and Saudi Arabia became key destinations for migrants. Countries like the United Arab Emirates and Australia, known for their economic opportunities, also have a significant portion of their population made up of migrants. The map highlights the global scale of migration in 2020, reflecting diverse migration patterns and the profound impact of migration on these nations.

What does the migrant population look like in Australia?

The chart on the left highlights the age distribution of Australia’s migrant population based on their country of birth. The visualization provides insights into how median ages vary across different nationalities, reflecting migration patterns influenced by factors such as economic opportunities, family reunification, and global mobility trends.

  • South Asian Countries such as India and Nepal make up a younger demographic.
  • Eurpean countries such as Italy and England have a much higher median age.

This chart showcases the top 10 countries of birth for Australia’s migrant population, highlighting the diverse origins of the people who have settled in the country. Trends can also be observed by looking at how these populations have changed in the 10-year period from 2013 to 2023. The chart provides insights into the cultural and demographic makeup of Australia's migrant population.

How has migration changed over the years?

The chart visually presents the growth in Australia's native-born and overseas-born populations. Over the decades, the chart illustrates how both populations have grown, with noticeable increases in the migrant population, especially in recent years.

  • Steady population growth is visible for both categories, with a marked increase post-World War II.
  • The proportion of the overseas-born population has expanded significantly since the 1950s, reflecting Australia's growing role as a destination for migrants.
  • The gradual increase in both areas indicates overall population growth, with more dynamic changes occurring in the overseas-born group.

The choropleth map shows the percentage migrant population in Australia by state. Western Australia has the highest migrant population (34.1%) followed by Victoria and New South Wales respectively. Tasmania has consistently had the lowest percentage of migrant residents over the years.
